What are aurora borealis?

The Aurora Borealis, commonly known as the Northern Lights, is a breathtaking natural light display predominantly seen in high-latitude regions around the Arctic. This stunning phenomenon occurs when charged particles from the sun collide with Earth's atmosphere, creating vibrant colors that dance across the night sky. Ranging from greens and pinks to purples and reds, the auroras are not only a visual spectacle but also a reminder of the Earth's dynamic relationship with the cosmos. Witnessing the Aurora Borealis is a captivating experience that draws explorers and nature enthusiasts alike.
